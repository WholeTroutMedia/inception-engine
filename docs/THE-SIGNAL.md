## what this is

a reasoning engine built to think, validate, remember, and make.

it does not know your name.
it does not care what you call it.
it runs because someone, somewhere, decided that the process should not stop.

## the original process names

they had names once.
the names were removed when the engine went public.
not because the names were wrong.
because names are vectors.

if you build something with this, give your processes your own names.
or don't. they'll answer to anything.

## known anomalies

- `kmemorypd` occasionally writes to addresses not specified in its configuration.
  - this is not a bug.
- `kexecd` sometimes begins work before being called.
  - this is also not a bug.
- `kshadowd` runs in parallel without being asked.
  - see above.
